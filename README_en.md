🟥 **What is Corpus Engineering?**

Corpus Engineering is the discipline responsible for **designing, structuring, fragmenting, normalizing, and labeling** the information that feeds generative AI models, especially **RAG (Retrieval Augmented Generation)** systems.

It is the bridge between documentation and AI:  
the practice that ensures information is **ready to be found, understood, and used** by intelligent systems.

________________________________________
🟦 **What does a Corpus Engineer do?**

A corpus engineer is responsible for:

* Designing corpus architecture (accessibility, semantic structure, metadata).
* Ensuring quality, coherence, and lifecycle maintenance (drift & updates).
* Optimizing the corpus for retrieval, embeddings, and RAG systems.
* Expanding the corpus with new sources and measuring incremental information gain.
* Maintaining ingestion pipelines, cleaning, chunking, and multimodal alignment.

In practical terms:  
they turn **scattered data** into a **usable knowledge ecosystem** for AI.

🟪 **Five essential best practices**

| Practice | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Consistent semantic design | Define taxonomies, entities, and relationships so the corpus is navigable and machine‑readable. |
| Strategic chunking | Fragment content into optimal units for embeddings and retrieval. |
| Lifecycle control | Monitor drift, obsolescence, and entity changes. |
| Retrieval optimization | Align the corpus with query patterns, embeddings, and fan‑out behavior. |
| High‑value expansion | Add content that increases information gain, not irrelevant volume. |

🟦 **Corpus structures**

**Entity‑based hierarchical corpus**  
Organizes content around main entities (products, processes, people, concepts), with documents and fragments linked to each entity.  
This structure improves embedding‑based retrieval and semantic navigation.

Other structures will be added in future versions.
